Ivonne
PI
Dr. Ivonne González-Gamboa is a postdoctoral research fellow at the Biomedical Engineering Group at Tec de Monterrey. Ivonne began to pursue a career in science while studying her bachelor degree in Biotechnology Engineering with a minor in Research and Innovation at Tec de Monterrey. She later obtained a Fundación Carolina Fellowship to do a M.Sc. in Agrobiotechnology at the Polytechnic University of Madrid.

In order to further develop the expertise needed to merge her knowledge in plant viruses and biomedical applications, she pursued a Ph.D. in Bionanotechnology, endorsed by a CONACYT Scholarship, under the direction of Dr. Fernando Ponz, graduating in September 2017 with a Cum Laude mention. Her research has been featured in high-ranked journals regarding bionanotechnology and its applications in diagnostics and immunization. Currently, her work is centered on developing chemically-modified viral nano meshes as biomaterials and doing 3D bioprinting of them for tissue regeneration.

Kalaumari Mayoral Peña (Biotechnology)
Advisor
Kalaumari received his B.S. in Biotechnology Engineering from Tecnologico de Monterrey and completed a master’s in Food Science and Technology at Autonomous University of Queretaro. During his master’s degree, Kalaumari worked in the development of electrochemical biosensors. Since 2016, he has been advisor and mentor for the Biomolecular Design Competition (BIOMOD) in Tecnologico de Monterrey. He is a PhD student in Biotechnology from this institution. He is doing his research in the Brigham and Women’s Hospital, Harvard Medical School and MIT. His current work is focused in the development of accessible diagnostic devices for early detection and monitoring of cancer and cardiovascular disease.
